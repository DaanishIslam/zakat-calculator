Zakat on Pure Gold and Gold Jewellery

Calculate Zakat at 2.5% of current market value (as of 1st Ramadan). Most scholars recommend using current market value rather than purchase price.

Zakat on Silver

Pay Zakat at 2.5% on silver in any form (jewelry, utensils, decorations). Use current market rates for valuation.

Zakat on Precious Stones

There's debate about including precious stones. If they hold value, they may be included in your wealth calculation. Consult a scholar for guidance as opinions vary.

Zakat on Cash and Bank Balances

Pay 2.5% Zakat on all cash and bank balances (savings, current, FD accounts). Ideally, the amount should be in your account for one lunar year.

Zakat on Loans and Investments

Include loans you've given in your Zakat calculation. Deduct loans you owe from your total wealth. Zakat applies to bonds, insurance premiums, and provident funds - consult a scholar about Sharia compliance.

Zakat on Property

No Zakat on personal residences or rental properties (only on rental income). Zakat applies if you hold property for investment/sale. If property use changes to business, include its value that year.

Zakat on Business

Business owners pay Zakat on all stock (valued at cost price). Include credit sales and deduct supplier debts. No Zakat on buildings/machinery, only on finished goods. Be honest in valuations as Zakat protects your wealth.

Zakat for Partnerships

Partners can pay Zakat individually or through the business. Calculate based on capital account plus estimated profit share. Use last balance sheet figures as reference.

Zakat on Agriculture

Pay Zakat on harvest (no one-year requirement). For multiple crops, pay Zakat for each harvest. Rates vary: 10% (rainwater), 5% (irrigation), or 7.5% (mixed).

Zakat on Animals

For grazing animals (goats, sheep, cows, camels): Pay 1 animal per 40 owned (or equivalent value). Consult local scholars for specific rulings.

Deducting Liabilities

Deduct: - Taxes due - Loans for Zakatable assets Don't deduct loans for personal items (houses, cars). Important: Avoid interest (riba) completely as it's prohibited in Islam.
